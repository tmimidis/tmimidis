Theodore "Teddy" Roosevelt

Naturalist, Conservationist, Politician.

The following timeline summarizes some of Teddy's life and history.

  • 1858 - Born on October 28th in Manhatten, New York City.
  • 1876 - Began attending Harvard University, where he was an accomplished naturalist, and published ornithologist. He was active in rowing and boxing.
  • 1880 - Graduates from Harvard Phi Beta Kappa (22nd out of 177). During this year he also marries his first wife, Alice Hathaway lee.
  • 1882 - Served as a member of the New york State assembly from 1882-1884 where he had an immediate impact on corporate corruption. He blocked efforts of financier Jay Gould to lower his own taxes and exposed suspected collusion by Judge Theodore Westbook which won him great popularity and secured his second election.
  • 1884 - Attends the 1884 National GOP Convenction where he gives a speech convincing to nominate African American John R. Lynch, a supporter of George F. Edmunds(Presidential Candidate), as the temporary chair. Retires from politics and moves to North Dakota where he lived the life of a Cowboy on his Elkhorn ranch.
  • 1886 - Returns to New York. Runs for Mayor but places 3rd. Writes his successful book "Winning of the West". Marries his second wife, Edith kermit Carow.
  • 1894 - Takes position as New York City Police Commissioner. Radically reforms the police force.
  • 1897Appointed Assistant Secretary of the Navy.
  • 1898 - Following the explosion of USS Maine in Havana, Cuba and the subsequent declaration of war on Spain, Roosevelt resigns from his position and forms the 1st US Volunteer Cavalry Regiment. Fights in Cuba where he is promoted to Colonel and recommended for the Medal of Honor for his actions at Kettle Hill. Following his return to the States, he successfully runs for Governor of New York.
  • 1899 - Roosevelt is nominated for Vice President under William McKinley. They proceed to win the Election and Roosevelt apprehensively takes up his new position.
  • 1901 McKinley is shot and wounded. Shortly after he dies of his wound and Teddy is sworn in as the 26th US President. He would serve for two terms from 1901-1908.
  • 1909 - Travels to Africa with the Smithsonian-Roosevelt Africa Expedition.
  • 1912 - Unsuccessfully runs for a 3rd Presidential term, while splitting the Republican party. During his campaign drive, he was shot in the chest by John Flammang Schrank. The bullet pierced a steel glasses case and a book before becoming lodged in Roosevelt's chest. He survived and was spared major injuries.
  • 1913 - Begins the Roosevelt-Rondon expedition to South America. During this expedition while mapping the Amazon River, Teddy injured his leg and developed Tropical Fever. He ended up losing 50lb and greatly diminishing his health. After finishing the expedition, he returned to the US and successfully defended his claims from the expedition.
  • 1914 - Endorses the allies at the onset of WW1. Supports harsher policy for Germany, and denounced Woodrow Wilson's foreign policy as a failure.
  • 1915 - Unveils his early plan for his version of "The League of Nations"
  • 1919 - Dies in his sleep due to a blood clot on January 5th, leaving a lasting and memorable legacy, including 6 children.
